## The Problem With Small Business Websites

Most small businesses fall into one of three traps with their website:

**The Ghost Town.** The site went up two years ago. The hours are wrong. The menu is from last fall. Nobody updates it because updating it is a whole thing. You have to remember your login, figure out the CMS, hope nothing breaks when you hit publish.

**The Expensive Middleman.** You pay a web developer or agency to maintain your site. Every change is a support ticket. Want to update your hours for the holidays? That'll be an email, a wait, and maybe a bill. The site looks great. Updating it feels like pulling teeth.

**The DIY Treadmill.** You use Squarespace, Wix, or WordPress. It works, technically. But you're spending hours every month fighting with templates, widgets, and formatting instead of running your business. And you're paying $15 to $50 a month for the privilege.

All three traps share the same root problem: there's friction between what you want on your website and getting it there.

## What If the Friction Just Disappeared?

Imagine this instead: you tell your AI agent "we're running a sourdough special this weekend, update the site." And it does. New content, correct formatting, live in seconds.

Or: "Add a blog post about our new cinnamon raisin loaf." Written, formatted, published. You review it on your phone while you're pulling bread out of the oven.

Or: "Our hours are changing for the summer. Monday through Friday, 7 to 3." Updated everywhere it appears on the site. Pushed live. Done before you finish your coffee.

No CMS. No developer. No monthly platform fee. Your agent knows your site, knows your brand, and handles the technical work so you never have to think about it.

## How It Actually Works

The technology behind this is surprisingly simple:

1. **Your website lives in a code repository** (think of it as a filing cabinet for your site's files). Hosting is free through GitHub Pages.
2. **Your AI agent has access to that repository.** It can read, edit, and publish changes.
3. **You talk to your agent** through whatever channel you already use. Slack, text, email. Tell it what you want.
4. **The agent makes the change and pushes it live.** GitHub rebuilds the site automatically. Your update is live in under a minute.

There's no monthly hosting fee. No proprietary platform locking you in. The site is yours, the files are yours, and if you ever want to move to a different setup, everything comes with you.

## What This Costs

Let's compare:

- **Web developer retainer:** $100 to $500 per month, plus per-change fees
- **Managed website platform (Squarespace, Wix, etc.):** $15 to $50 per month
- **WordPress hosting + maintenance:** $20 to $100 per month
- **AI-managed static site on GitHub Pages:** $0 for hosting. Your only cost is the agent service itself.

The website becomes a feature of your AI agent, not a separate line item. The same agent that handles your email, your scheduling, and your social media also keeps your website current.
