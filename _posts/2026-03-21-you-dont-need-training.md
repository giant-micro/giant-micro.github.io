Here's a number that should bother the entire AI industry: 76% of small businesses are now using AI. But only 14% have fully integrated it into their operations.

That's not early adoption. That's a 62-point gap between "tried it" and "it works." Seventy-three percent of those businesses say they need more training and resources. And every AI consultant in the world just heard a cash register.

But they're hearing it wrong.

## The Training Trap

When someone says "I need training," what they usually mean is: "This thing isn't working the way I expected, and I assume it's my fault."

It's not.

You don't need a workshop on prompt engineering. You don't need a webinar about "AI strategy." You don't need a consultant to spend three months building you a roadmap that sits in a drawer.

You already tried AI. You signed up for the tool. You watched the demo. You maybe even got it to do something useful once or twice. Then it stopped being useful because nobody was there to keep it running, connect it to your other systems, or adapt it when your needs changed.

That's not a knowledge gap. That's a completion gap.

## Where We're Going, You Won't Need Training

Think about the last tool that actually worked for you. Maybe it was Square for payments, or QuickBooks for accounting, or even just your phone's calendar. Did you take a training course? Read a manual? Hire a consultant?

No. You opened it, it made sense, and it did what it was supposed to do.

That's the bar. And AI hasn't cleared it for small businesses because the industry is obsessed with selling you the engine and expecting you to build the car.

We think differently. We think your AI should show up ready to work. It should know your business. It should plug into the tools you already use. And when something needs to change, someone should handle that for you.

Not a chatbot. Not a dashboard. A team member that happens to be software.

## The Real Number

That 73% asking for "training and resources"? Those aren't people who need to learn more. Those are people who've been let down by tools that promised simplicity and delivered complexity.

They don't need training. They need someone to make it work and keep it working.

That's what we do.
